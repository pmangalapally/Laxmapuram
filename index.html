Welcome to Laxmapuram

Laxmapuram (లక్ష్మాపురం) is a vibrant village nestled in Ramannapet Mandal of Yadadri Bhuvanagiri District, Telangana. Located approximately 70 km from Hyderabad, the village is home to around 2,000–3,000 residents living across 400–600 households.

The village draws its name from Goddess Lakshmi, the Hindu deity of wealth and prosperity, and is presided over by the revered Sri Laxminarasimha Swamy — a combined form of Lakshmi and Narasimha (the fourth avatar of Lord Vishnu). This divine heritage shapes the spiritual character and cultural identity of the community.

Life in Laxmapuram revolves around agriculture, faith, and strong community bonds. Fields of rice, cotton, and pulses stretch across the fertile terrain, sustained by a historic village tank (cheruvu) and modern bore wells. The village benefits from Mission Bhagiratha piped water supply, 24×7 electricity, and good road connectivity to Ramannapet (~5 km) and beyond.

Village Landmarks

The heart and soul of Laxmapuram

🏛️

Gram Panchayat Office

The administrative heart of the village, where local governance and community decisions are made for Laxmapuram.

💧

Village Tank (Cheruvu)

The historic village tank has been the lifeline of farmers for generations, providing irrigation for surrounding agricultural lands.

🌳

Pedda Chetu (Banyan Tree)

The ancient village banyan tree — a gathering place for elders, a natural shade for travellers, and a living symbol of the village's roots.

👶

Anganwadi Centre

The village's early childhood care and education centre, serving children and mothers under ICDS schemes with nutrition and health support.

🏫

ZP Primary School

The Zilla Parishad Primary School has been nurturing young minds of Laxmapuram for decades, providing foundational education.

🛣️

Ramannapet Road

The main arterial road connecting Laxmapuram to Ramannapet town (~5 km) and to the broader highway network toward Hyderabad.

Sacred Temples

Places of devotion and spiritual heritage

🛕

Sri Laxminarasimha Swamy Temple

Presiding deity of the village. The combined form of Lakshmi & Narasimha — from whom the village takes its name.

🙏

Sri Rama Temple

Dedicated to Lord Rama, this temple is especially vibrant during Ram Navami celebrations every spring.

🌺

Yellamma / Pochamma Devi Temple

The Grama Devata (village goddess) temple — Pochamma Devi is worshipped as the village's protective mother deity.

🔱

Shiva Temple

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple draws devotees during Maha Shivaratri for night-long prayers and rituals.

✨

Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy, Yadagirigutta

A famous regional temple ~20 km away at Yadagirigutta — an important pilgrimage site for villagers.

Festivals & Celebrations

Year-round culture, colour & community spirit

🌸
March / April

Ugadi

Telugu New Year celebrated with Ugadi Pachadi — a mix of six tastes symbolising the flavours of life. Families reunite and seek blessings.

🪁
January

Makar Sankranti

The harvest festival of Telangana — kite flying, Haridasu blessings, bonfire (Bhogi), and special sweets like Ariselu and Pongal.

🥁
July / August

Bonalu

The grand Telangana festival celebrating the mother goddess. Women carry pots of prasadam in colourful processions to the temple.

🌼
Sept / Oct

Bathukamma

The unique Telangana flower festival — women stack seasonal flowers into a beautiful tower and sing folk songs around it.
